The B.A. in Psychology degree is designed to provide students with a sufficient background in psychology to pursue degrees in a variety of professions as well as graduate schools in certain fields. This degree also affords students the opportunity to specialize in other areas through minors, concentration, and Certificates of Undergraduate Studies (CUGS).

Potential Careers

  • Clinical Psychologist*Earn upwards of $82,180
  • Counseling- Mental Health/Substance AbuseEarn upwards of $47,660
  • Industrial-Organizational Psychologist*Earn upwards of $96,270
  • School Psychologist*Earn upwards of $87,590

*Requires education beyond bachelor's Wage data from BLS.gov and O*NET OnLine

Courses and Program Guide

All Rowan Partnership Online programs have been designed to be delivered in an convenient online format. These courses are designed to help you get the information you need at a pace you'll love.

Accelerating Your Learning

Many Rowan Partnership Online courses offer a 7-week format for courses as opposed to a traditional 15 week format. Rather than taking five or six courses all at the same time, you can take two or three and go further in-depth and get the education you deserve.
